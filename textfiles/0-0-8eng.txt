Cheon ja chong tong: Length: 130cm, Diameter: 13cm, Weight: 296kg, Dating from the Joseon Dynasty (1555), it is the oldest cannon preserved in Korea and was used to fire shots by loading it with the general's fire arrow and *suchul-yeonuihwan. This Cheonjachongtong is preserved and exhibited in the Army Museum as Treasure No. 647. *Suchul-yeonuihwan: an iron casting technique where molten iron is poured into a mold and then lead is added to it.
