Nummulite: A type of fossilized foraminifera that thrived during the Paleogene Period, from 65 million to 2.5 million years ago. Nummulite is a standardized fossil of the Paleogene, belonging to the larger species among foraminifera and resembling a flattened coin. It is called Nummulite in Latin, meaning "small coin," due to its coin-like shape. Its internal structure consists of numerous chambers divided by septa, with the chambers interconnected through narrow passages.
